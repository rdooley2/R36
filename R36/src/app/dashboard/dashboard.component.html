Recent Innovations in Clean Energy

U.S. Energy Production Climbing Faster than Energy Consumption

According to the Energy Information Administration, EIA for short, energy production in the United States has steadily increased faster than energy consumption over the past fifty years. In 1974, the United States was consuming more energy than it could produce. To solve this, the United States relied on energy imports (buying energy from other countries). As you might expect, buying energy from other countries is not the most viable solution to the energy consumption problem. Over time, the United States has slowly begun to make more energy than we can consume, they even sell it to other countries now. As of 2024, energy production in the United States is up sixty-eight percent compared to that of 1974. While nonrenewable energy source consumption has increased due to new methods such as hydraulic fracking and horizontal drilling, innovations in clean energy have also made a significant impact. Since the 1990s the United States has steadily been investing more into methods such as wind, solar, and hydropower. For example, solar energy has steadily been increasing over the past two decades. More of this specific example can be seen in the summary and report sections. Clean and Renewable Energy make up approximately 11 percent of all United States energy consumption and 17.6 percent of energy generation as of 2024.

Source: Energy Information Administration (EIA)

Technical Overview:

As per the project instructions, my project utilizes Angular for the Frontend and Node.js with Express for the Backend. The Backend uses MongoDB (Mongoose) to store chart data. The Frontend is served with NGINX running on port 80 and the Backend running on port 3000. Authentication for the Login Page is provided through the use of JSON Web Tokens (JWT). The Frontend also includes dynamic charts which are populated by endpoints in the Backend that fetch data from the MongoDB Database. Finally, the project incorporates ADA/WCAG principles in the Frontend code.
